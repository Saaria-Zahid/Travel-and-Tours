Exploring The Badshahi Mosque

The Badshahi Mosque is a Mughal-era congregational mosque in Lahore, capital of the Pakistani province of Punjab, Pakistan.

Overview

The Badshahi Mosque is a Mughal-era congregational mosque in Lahore, capital of the Pakistani province of Punjab, Pakistan. The mosque is located west of Lahore Fort along the outskirts of the Walled City of Lahore, and is widely considered to be one of Lahore's most iconic landmarks. The Badshahi Mosque was constructed by the Mughal emperor Aurangzeb between 1671 and 1673 and was the largest mosque in the world from 1673 to 1986. The mosque is an important example of Mughal architecture, with an exterior that is decorated with carved red sandstone with marble inlay. It remains the largest mosque of the Mughal-era, and is the third-largest mosque in Pakistan. After the fall of the Mughal Empire, the mosque was used as a garrison by the British Empire, and is now one of Pakistan's most iconic sights.

Badshahi Highlights

Don't miss these mind-boggling ruins within Badshahi Mosque:

  • Intihuatana - hitching post of the sun
  • Sacred Plaza
  • Main Gate (original entrance) & Quarries
  • Dry Moat & Long Staircase
  • Temple of the Sun & Royal Tomb
  • Mortars & Temple of the Condor

Walking beyond the main ruins, you can escape the crowds and explore:

  • The climb to the top of Badshahi Mosque and the Sacred Rock at the entrance (must reserve well in advance)
  • The Inca bridge crossing a sheer cliff
  • The Gate of the Sun, entrance above Badshahi Mosque where the Inca Trail enters the citadel.
